The Perfect Weekend Getaway from Coimbatore — Why Wild Grove Should Be Your First Choice

📅 June 15, 2026✍️ By Madhu Ravindran⏱️ 6 min read

If you live in Coimbatore and you've been telling yourself, "I need a break," then keep reading. Because at just 1.5 hours from the city — tucked away in the Sholayur hills of Attapadi — there's a place that feels like a different world entirely.

Why Not Just Go to Ooty?

Let's be honest. Ooty is beautiful, but it's also crowded, commercialized, and the drive can take 3-4 hours in peak season. What if there was a place closer, quieter, and more authentic? That's Wild Grove.

Perched atop a scenic hill overlooking a sweeping valley, Wild Grove Organic Farm & Resort offers something rare — genuine silence. No honking, no traffic, no tourist buses. Just the hum of windmills, birdsong, and the rustle of leaves.

The Drive: A Journey in Itself

The route from Coimbatore via Anaikatti Road is spectacular. As the city fades behind you, the Western Ghats unfold in layers of green. You'll pass through small villages, catch glimpses of wildlife, and feel the temperature drop as you climb. The last 20 minutes from Anaikatti to Sholayur are pure magic — hairpin bends with valley views on both sides.

What Makes Wild Grove Different

  • It's a working organic farm — not just a hotel with a garden. You'll walk through rows of vegetables, spice plants, and fruit trees that actually feed you during your stay.
  • The stays are unique — from an Instagram-worthy A-Frame cottage to a traditional Kerala cottage and a spacious three-bedroom villa with a private deck.
  • The food is unforgettable — traditional Kerala sadya, farm-fresh vegetables, homestyle cooking that tastes like grandmother's kitchen.
  • The activities are real — night jeep safaris, off-road adventures, astrophotography classes, trekking, and a hilltop pool with infinity views.
"We came for one night and wished we'd booked three. The A-Frame cottage, the stargazing, and the Kerala food — unforgettable." — A recent guest

The Perfect 2-Night Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive & Unwind

Check in at 2:00 PM. Settle into your cottage. Take a slow walk through the organic farm. Evening tea on the deck with valley views. Bonfire dinner under the stars.

Day 2: Adventure Day

Morning swim in the hilltop pool. Post-breakfast organic farming class. Afternoon off-road jeep safari through the Western Ghats. Night jeep safari to spot wildlife.

Day 3: Slow Morning

Sunrise yoga on the deck. Leisurely Kerala breakfast. Check out at 11:00 AM with memories (and homemade jam) to take home.

Organic Farming at Wild Grove — How We Nurture the Land That Nurtures You

📅 May 28, 2026✍️ By Madhu Ravindran⏱️ 7 min read

When I left my 20-year career in IT Security to start Wild Grove, people thought I was crazy. "Why would you leave a comfortable corporate job to dig soil on a hilltop?" they asked. The answer was simple — I wanted to live honestly. And honest living starts with honest food, grown in honest soil.

Today, Wild Grove's organic farm is the beating heart of our resort. Every meal our guests eat begins here — in these terraced beds, under these windmills, in this rich red earth of Sholayur.

Why Organic? Why Now?

India's soil is in crisis. Decades of chemical farming have depleted nutrients, killed beneficial microorganisms, and poisoned groundwater. The food on most supermarket shelves is grown with synthetic pesticides and fertilizers that harm both the environment and our health.

At Wild Grove, we chose a different path. We farm the way our grandparents did — with nature, not against it. The result? Soil that gets richer every year, produce that tastes the way food is supposed to taste, and an ecosystem that thrives around us.

Our Core Farming Practices

1. Composting & Vermicomposting

Every bit of organic waste on the farm — kitchen scraps, fallen leaves, cow dung, crop residue — goes into our compost pits. We maintain three stages: fresh waste, decomposing material, and ready-to-use humus. Our vermicompost beds house thousands of red wriggler earthworms that transform waste into black gold — nutrient-rich vermicompost that our plants love.

2. Crop Rotation & Companion Planting

We never plant the same crop in the same bed consecutively. Legumes follow heavy feeders, leafy greens follow root vegetables. This keeps the soil balanced and naturally replenishes nitrogen. We also practice companion planting — marigolds next to tomatoes to repel pests, basil alongside peppers to attract pollinators, and lemongrass as a natural mosquito barrier around the property.

3. Natural Pest Management

Instead of reaching for chemical pesticides, we use:

  • Neem oil spray — a natural insecticide and fungicide made from our own neem trees
  • Panchagavya — a traditional bio-fertilizer made from five cow products (milk, curd, ghee, urine, and dung)
  • Jivamrutha — a microbial culture that boosts soil biology
  • Predator insects — we encourage ladybugs, lacewings, and spiders that naturally control pest populations
  • Physical barriers — net houses for seedlings, sticky traps for whiteflies

4. Mulching & Water Conservation

Our farm sits on a hilltop where water is precious. We use thick layers of dried leaf mulch and coconut husk around every plant to retain soil moisture, suppress weeds, and regulate soil temperature. Drip irrigation delivers water directly to roots with minimal waste. Rainwater harvesting systems capture monsoon runoff into storage tanks.

5. Seed Saving

We save seeds from our best-performing plants each season. This means our crops are naturally adapting to the specific conditions of Sholayur's soil, altitude, and climate — becoming more resilient with each generation. We never use hybrid or GMO seeds.

What We Grow

Our farm produces over 30 varieties across the year:

  • Vegetables: Tomatoes, brinjal, okra, beans, drumstick, spinach, bottle gourd, bitter gourd, ash gourd, pumpkin, chow-chow, tapioca
  • Greens: Amaranth, moringa leaves, curry leaves, fenugreek, mint, coriander
  • Spices: Turmeric, ginger, black pepper, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon
  • Fruits: Banana, papaya, jackfruit, mango, coconut, passion fruit
  • Specialty crops: Coffee, cocoa, lemongrass, vetiver (for erosion control)

From Farm to Your Plate

The distance from our farm to the kitchen is less than 100 meters. There's no cold storage, no transportation, no preservatives. The vegetables you eat at lunch were picked that morning. The curry leaves in your sambar were plucked minutes before they hit the pan. The coconut in your chutney was cracked open that day.

This is what farm-to-table really means — and you can taste the difference in every single bite.

"I've eaten at Michelin-starred restaurants, but the sadya at Wild Grove — made from vegetables harvested that morning — was the most flavourful meal I've had. You can't fake freshness." — Suresh, guest from Mumbai

Join Our Farming Class

Every guest at Wild Grove is welcome to walk the farm, ask questions, and get their hands dirty. Our structured farming class includes:

  • A guided walk through every section of the farm with our head farmer
  • Hands-on experience with composting and vermicomposting
  • Learning about natural pest management and Panchagavya preparation
  • Planting a sapling that we'll nurture after you leave
  • Harvesting vegetables that go straight into your next meal

Children absolutely love it — pulling up carrots, spotting earthworms, and understanding where food really comes from. It's one of our most requested activities.

Our Vision: Beyond the Farm

Wild Grove isn't just about growing food for our guests. We're building a model for sustainable hill agriculture that can be replicated. We work with local tribal farmers in Attapadi, sharing seeds, techniques, and knowledge. We dream of a day when every resort in the Western Ghats grows its own food organically — because the hills deserve better than chemicals.

25 Stunning Birds You Can Spot in Attapadi — A Birdwatcher's Paradise at Wild Grove

📅 May 10, 2026✍️ By Madhu Ravindran⏱️ 8 min read

If you're a birdwatcher — or even mildly curious about birds — Attapadi should be on your bucket list. Nestled within the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve, the Sholayur hills where Wild Grove Resort sits are part of one of the world's most biodiverse regions. Over 170 species of birds have been recorded in the Attapadi region, including several rare Western Ghats endemics that you simply won't find elsewhere.

Here's your complete guide to the birds you can spot right from our resort — and on the trails around Sholayur.

🦅 Raptors & Birds of Prey

The thermals rising from the Attapadi valley make it a paradise for raptors. Look up, and you'll often see them circling.

  • Crested Serpent Eagle — the most commonly seen raptor here. Its distinctive whistling call echoes through the valleys. You'll often spot it perched on tall trees scanning for snakes below.
  • Shaheen Falcon — the Indian subspecies of the Peregrine Falcon. Rare and magnificent, occasionally seen hunting at dawn from the cliff faces near Sholayur.
  • Black Eagle — look for its broad, fingered wings and slow soaring flight over the canopy. One of the most majestic sights in the Western Ghats.
  • Brahminy Kite — chestnut and white, unmistakable. Often seen gliding over the river valleys below the resort.

🦜 Hornbills & Large Forest Birds

The dense forests around Attapadi are home to some of India's most spectacular large birds.

  • Malabar Grey Hornbill — a Western Ghats endemic! These charismatic birds are frequently seen around Wild Grove, hopping through fig trees with their distinctive curved bills. Their laughing calls are one of the signature sounds of our resort.
  • Great Indian Hornbill — enormous, with a wingspan of 5 feet and a bright yellow casque. Rare but occasionally spotted in the deep forests near Sholayur. Hearing its wingbeats overhead — a deep, rhythmic whooshing — is an unforgettable experience.
  • Indian Grey Hornbill — smaller and more common than its Malabar cousin, but equally delightful to watch as it tosses berries into the air and catches them.

🐦 Western Ghats Endemics

These birds are found only in the Western Ghats — nowhere else on Earth. Spotting even a few of these makes any birder's trip worthwhile.

  • Nilgiri Flycatcher — a stunning indigo-blue bird with a soft, melancholic call. Found in shola forests above 1000m. Best spotted during early morning walks along the ridge trail.
  • White-bellied Blue Flycatcher — deep blue with a clean white belly. Skulks in the undergrowth but will perch in the open briefly, rewarding patient observers.
  • Malabar Parakeet (Blue-winged Parakeet) — a gorgeous green-and-blue parakeet with a red bill. Moves in noisy flocks through the canopy — you'll hear them before you see them.
  • Flame-throated Bulbul — olive-green with a striking orange-red throat. Endemic to the Western Ghats and a common garden visitor at Wild Grove.
  • White-bellied Treepie — a member of the crow family but far more elegant, with a long graduated tail and rufous-brown plumage. Often seen hopping through branches near our Kerala Cottage.
  • Crimson-backed Sunbird — tiny, jewel-like, with iridescent crimson and purple plumage. The males glitter like living gemstones as they feed on flowers around the resort.
  • Nilgiri Woodpigeon — large, handsome, and shy. Its deep booming call resonates through the morning mist in the shola patches above Sholayur.
  • White-cheeked Barbet — plump, green, and always calling — "kutruk-kutruk-kutruk." One of the easiest endemics to spot, often seen in fruiting trees near the villa.
"I came to Wild Grove for a relaxing weekend. I left as a birdwatcher. The Malabar Grey Hornbill sitting in the jackfruit tree right outside my cottage window at sunrise — I'll never forget that moment." — Kavitha, guest from Chennai

🦉 Owls & Nocturnal Birds

The forests around Wild Grove come alive after dark with nocturnal species. Our night safari provides excellent opportunities to spot them.

  • Mottled Wood Owl — large, buff-and-brown, with a haunting "hu-hu-hu-hoo" call that echoes through the night. Sometimes spotted roosting in large trees during the day.
  • Indian Scops Owl — tiny and perfectly camouflaged. Its trembling whistle call is a common night sound at Wild Grove.
  • Sri Lanka Frogmouth — one of the most sought-after birds in the Western Ghats! Extremely well-camouflaged and resembling a broken branch. Our guides know a few reliable roosting spots — ask during your stay!
  • Jerdon's Nightjar — seen hawking insects over open areas at dusk. Its churring song is part of the Sholayur soundscape every evening.

🌿 Common Residents You'll See Every Day

Even without going on a dedicated birding walk, you'll spot these species just sitting on your cottage veranda:

  • Indian Roller — electric blue wings flash as it dives from its perch
  • Black-hooded Oriole — bright golden-yellow with a jet-black hood
  • White-browed Wagtail — dainty, constantly bobbing its tail by the pool
  • Greater Coucal — deep, booming calls at dawn; glossy black and chestnut
  • Purple Sunbird — males shimmer metallic purple in sunlight
  • Indian Paradise Flycatcher — the male's long, streaming white tail ribbons are unmistakable

🗓️ Best Time for Birdwatching at Wild Grove

Birds are here year-round, but different seasons offer different highlights:

  • October – February (Winter) — Best overall. Resident birds are joined by winter migrants from the Himalayas and Central Asia. Cool mornings make for comfortable walks. Peak activity 6:00 – 9:00 AM.
  • March – May (Summer) — Breeding season. Males in full plumage, active display calls. Great for photography. Listen for hornbill calls at dawn.
  • June – September (Monsoon) — Lush greenery, fewer visitors. Some species are harder to spot in the rain, but the forest is magical. Frogmouths and nightjars more vocal.

🎒 What to Bring for Birding

  • Binoculars — 8x42 or 10x42 are ideal (we can lend a pair on request)
  • Field guide — "Birds of the Indian Subcontinent" by Grimmett, Inskipp & Inskipp
  • Camera with zoom lens — 200mm minimum, 400mm+ ideal
  • Muted clothing — greens, browns, greys (avoid bright colors)
  • Patience and silence — the two most important tools any birder can carry

Farm-to-Table: How We Grow Your Food at Wild Grove Resort

📅 April 22, 2026✍️ By Smitha Ravindran⏱️ 5 min read

When we say "farm-to-table," we mean it literally. The distance from our organic farm to your dining table at Wild Grove Resort? Less than 100 meters. No trucks, no warehouses, no preservatives. Just soil, sunshine, love, and your plate.

The Kerala Kitchen

Our kitchen is run by local cooks who've been making traditional Kerala food for decades. Every meal is a celebration of seasonal, local, organic ingredients. Here's what a typical day tastes like:

Breakfast

Puttu & kadala curry, appam & stew, dosa with coconut chutney, fresh fruit — all made from scratch that morning. The coconut is grated from our own trees. The curry leaves were picked minutes before they hit the pan.

Lunch

Kerala sadya-style meal — served on a banana leaf with rice, sambar, rasam, avial, thoran, pickle, and payasam for dessert. Every component uses ingredients from our farm or sourced from neighboring organic farmers.

Dinner

Homestyle Kerala dinner — chapati with vegetable curry, fish curry (for non-veg guests), or a hearty dal with farm-fresh vegetables. Finished with a warm cup of jaggery tea.
